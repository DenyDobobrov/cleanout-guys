The Complete Guide to Estate Cleanouts in Chicago

Clearing out a loved one's home is one of the hardest tasks a family faces. This guide walks through every step — practically and compassionately.

What Is an Estate Cleanout?

An estate cleanout is the process of clearing out a home's contents after a death, a major move, or a significant life transition like moving to assisted living. It typically involves sorting and removing furniture, clothing, kitchen items, garage contents, and accumulated belongings — often from a home lived in for decades.

Unlike a regular junk removal job, estate cleanouts require patience, care, and an understanding that many items have emotional significance to the family.

Step 1 — Give Yourself Time

If the estate involves the loss of a loved one, there's no rush in the first days after. Most families benefit from waiting at least a week or two before beginning the cleanout process. Rushing leads to regret about items that were discarded hastily.

Step 2 — Gather the Family First

Before anything is removed from the home, bring together family members who may want to claim personal items — jewelry, photos, heirlooms, furniture. This conversation is much easier when it happens before the cleanout rather than after. Consider creating a shared list in advance of who wants what.

Step 3 — Document Before You Remove

Walk through every room with your phone and take photos and video before sorting begins. This serves two purposes: it creates a record for sentimental purposes, and it can be useful documentation if there are any estate legal or probate matters.

Step 4 — Sort Into Categories

The classic estate cleanout system involves five piles:

  • Family keep — claimed by specific family members
  • Estate sale — valuable enough to sell
  • Donate — good condition, going to charity
  • Haul/Discard — too worn, broken, or low-value to keep or sell
  • Shred/Secure — financial documents, medical records, personal papers

Step 5 — Consider an Estate Sale

If there are significant valuables — antique furniture, collectibles, jewelry, art — an estate sale or auction may be worth considering before the cleanout. Estate sale companies in Chicago typically work on a 30–40% commission and handle all setup, pricing, and selling. Many will even coordinate with junk removal companies to haul away what doesn't sell.

Step 6 — Handle the Paperwork Safely

Before discarding anything with personal information, sort documents carefully. Financial statements, tax returns, medical records, and mail with personal information should be shredded. Consider renting a shredder or using a professional document shredding service for large volumes.

Step 7 — Schedule the Haul-Away

Once family items are claimed, the estate sale is complete (if applicable), and donations are sorted, it's time to clear what remains. For most Chicago-area homes, this means a professional junk removal crew — the volume is typically too large for regular trash pickup.

How Much Does an Estate Cleanout Cost in Chicago?

Estate cleanout pricing varies widely depending on the size of the home and volume of contents. Typical ranges in Chicago:

  • Studio or 1-bedroom: $300–$600
  • 2–3 bedroom home: $500–$1,200
  • Large home or home with full basement/garage: $1,000–$2,500+

Most reputable companies offer a free on-site estimate. Be wary of companies that quote over the phone without seeing the job.
