How Much Does Junk Removal Cost in Chicago? (2026 Guide)

Stop guessing. Here are the real numbers for junk removal pricing in Chicago — single items, partial loads, full truckloads, and everything that affects the final bill.

The Short Answer

Junk removal in Chicago typically costs $100–$600 for residential jobs, with the average single-family home cleanout running around $250–$400. Prices vary based on how much stuff you have, what it is, and where you're located.

Junk Removal Price Breakdown (Chicago, 2025)

Load Size Typical Price Range Best For
Single item $75–$150 One couch, appliance, or mattress
⅛ Truckload $100–$175 Small pile, a few boxes

½ Truckload $250–$400 Average garage cleanout
¾ Truckload $375–$500 Large cleanout or multiple rooms

What Factors Affect the Price?

Volume is the biggest driver — most companies charge by how much space your junk takes up in the truck. Beyond that:

  • Heavy items: Concrete, soil, and tile cost more to haul due to weight limits.
  • Difficult access: Basement or attic removals take more labor and time.
  • Hazardous materials: Paint, chemicals, and electronics require special handling and cost more.
  • Same-day service: Rush jobs may carry a small premium depending on the company.
  • Location: Suburbs farther from Chicago may have a small travel fee.

What's Included in the Price?

A reputable junk removal company like Garage Cleanout Guys includes the following at no extra charge:

  • Labor to carry items from any floor of your home
  • Loading into the truck
  • Disposal fees
  • A quick sweep of the area after pickup
  • Eco-friendly sorting for donation and recycling

Always confirm before booking — some companies charge separately for labor, fuel, or disposal.

Junk Removal vs. Dumpster Rental in Chicago

Dumpster rentals in Chicago typically run $350–$600 for a 10–15 yard container, plus permit fees if it sits on the street. For most residential jobs, full-service junk removal is more cost-effective and requires no permit, no waiting, and no loading yourself.

Bottom line: For most Chicago homeowners, a professional junk removal crew is cheaper, faster, and far less work than renting a dumpster.

How to Get the Best Price on Junk Removal

  1. Get an on-site quote — phone estimates are rough; an in-person look is more accurate.
  2. Consolidate your items before the crew arrives to minimize truck space.
  3. Book weekday appointments — some companies charge more on weekends.
  4. Ask about multi-load discounts if you have a large job.
  5. Confirm what's included upfront — labor, disposal, and cleanup should all be in the price.
